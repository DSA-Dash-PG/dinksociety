How it works

A circuit, a standing, and a story.

The Dink Society runs in Circuits — each one a seven-week social league with real competition, real standings, and a drink afterward. Here's how a Circuit runs.

01 · Format

Round-robin into a gold-medal final.

Each Circuit runs six teams per division over seven weeks. Six weeks of round-robin, then championship week. Every match feeds your division standings, your stats, and your Circuit Points.

02 · A night at the Society

Two rounds. Twelve games. Four points on the line.

Each league night is one match across two rounds, with six games per round. Games go to 11, win by 1. Whoever wins the most games takes the round. Four round points up for grabs.

  • :00
    Arrive & exchange lineups

    Captains trade written lineups ten minutes before play.

  • :15
    Round 1 · Six games

    Women's doubles, men's doubles, then four mixed games. 2 points for the win, 1 for a tie.

  • 1:00
    Round 2 · Six games

    Sides flip. Same structure. Another 2 points on the line.

  • 1:45
    Sign the scoresheet & stay

    Both captains sign off. Scores go into the Circuit leaderboard. Then it's the after-court round.

03 · Circuit points

Your finish follows you across Circuits.

Circuit Points are the Society's long game. Each Circuit, final placement banks points toward your all-time standing — and weekly bonuses stack on top. One Circuit is a season. The Circuit leaderboard is your legacy.
