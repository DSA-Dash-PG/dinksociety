How it works

Rules of the Society.

How matches are played, how scores are tallied, and the league policies every captain should know.

01 · Match format

Two rounds. Twelve games. Four points on the line.

Every league match is played across two rounds of six games each. Each round includes one women's doubles, one men's doubles, and four mixed doubles. Twelve games total per match, no exceptions.

G1
Women's doubles
F + F
G2
Men's doubles
M + M
G3
Mixed doubles
1 F + 1 M
G4
Mixed doubles
1 F + 1 M
G5
Mixed doubles
1 F + 1 M
G6
Mixed doubles
1 F + 1 M

Round 2 repeats the same game types in the same order. This structure is fixed — you cannot swap in a second women's doubles or drop a mixed slot.

Home and away roles

  • Round 1: Home serves first, away chooses side.
  • Round 2: Home chooses side, away serves first.

Roles flip between rounds so neither team keeps a positional advantage for the whole match.

02 · Gameplay

Games go to 11, win by 1.

Rally scoring. The first side to reach 11 points wins the game, provided they're ahead by at least one point. No cap, no win-by-two.

Switch sides at 6

Players switch ends of the court when the first side reaches 6 points. This balances sun, wind, and lighting conditions across both halves of the game.

Standard pickleball rules apply

All USA Pickleball rules govern play unless specifically modified here. Two-bounce rule, non-volley zone, service rotation — all as you'd expect. If you're unsure on a point, call the other captain over and work it out courtside.

Pace matters. Most of our venues give us a time window. Keep transitions brisk, exchange lineups ten minutes before start, and don't stretch into other teams' court time.
03 · Lineups

Lock early. Reveal together.

Before every match, both captains build a 12-game lineup: six games for Round 1 and six for Round 2. Each game gets two players from your team's roster.

Slot rules

  • Women's doubles (G1): Both players must be women.
  • Men's doubles (G2): Both players must be men.
  • Mixed doubles (G3–G6): Exactly one man and one woman.

The captain portal enforces these strictly. You cannot lock a lineup with a missing gender or a wrong-gender combo.

No back-to-back same pair within a round

The same two players cannot appear together in consecutive games within the same round. They can play together once in Round 1 and once in Round 2, or twice non-consecutively within a round — but not G3 then G4.

Hidden simultaneous reveal

When you lock your lineup, you see your own picks but nothing about the opponent. Theirs stay hidden until both captains have locked. Then the full matchup reveals simultaneously — no tactical advantage to going first or last.

Locking is final

Once both captains lock, lineups cannot be changed. If a player is injured in warm-up or doesn't show, contact an admin. There is no in-app late-swap for Circuit I.

04 · Scoring

Both captains enter. Both confirm.

Scoring is dual-entry. Both captains type their own version of each game's score into the portal. The system validates per-game agreement:

  • Scores match: Game is confirmed (green).
  • Scores differ: Mismatch surfaced (red). Captains resolve courtside — both re-type the correct numbers and the system auto-confirms.

Round points

At the end of each round, whoever won more games takes the round:

Win the round (more games won)
2 pts
Tie the round (3–3 split)
1 pt
Lose the round (fewer games won)
0 pts

Ties split 1–1. Maximum 2 points per round, 4 points per match.

Finalizing the match

A match is final only when all 12 games are confirmed AND both captains have tapped "Submit final." Any score edit after submission automatically withdraws both submissions — so honest mistakes never get locked in by accident. Both captains have to deliberately re-submit.

No admin-in-the-loop. If the two captains can't agree on a score and neither will change their entry, the match stays unfinalized and an admin will reach out. Please don't let this happen — it's always a miscount that can be resolved courtside.

Standings tiebreakers

If two teams tie on match points at Circuit end, the following tiebreakers apply in order:

  1. Total games won across the Circuit
  2. Head-to-head match points between the tied teams
  3. Point differential (match points for minus match points against)
06 · Conduct

Play hard. Stay human.

The Dink Society is recreational. People showed up to get some dinks in after work, not to get lawyered to death over a line call. Defaults:

  • Self-officiate. No refs. Players make their own line calls. When in genuine doubt, the ball was in — call it against yourself.
  • Pace and grace. Keep the games moving. Brief sportsmanlike back-and-forth is fine; extended gamesmanship is not.
  • Captain handles escalations. If a disagreement needs to be handled, the two captains talk it out. Players on the court stay out of it once the captains are involved.
  • Roster substitutes. You can only play players who are on your team's roster. No guest ringers. If someone's injured and you need to add a player, talk to an admin first.
Repeated unsportsmanlike conduct can get a team removed from the Circuit with no refund of dues. We've never had to do this and would prefer to keep it that way.
07 · Disputes

Resolve it at the court.

Almost every "dispute" is a scoring disagreement or a rule question. Both have straightforward resolutions:

Scoring disagreement

If the two captains' scores don't match on a game, the system shows a mismatch pill. Talk to each other courtside, figure out the right number, both re-type. Don't try to out-stubborn each other — no one wins.

Rule question mid-game

Pause the point. Both captains check the rule. If it's not obvious, replay the rally. Move on.

Anything that needs an admin

Email the league or DM @dinksociety.pb. Admins can reopen finalized matches, correct roster issues, and resolve things that captains genuinely can't settle. Please actually try to resolve at the court first — we want admins spending their time running the league, not triaging score disputes.
