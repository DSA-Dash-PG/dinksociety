01 · Team rosters

Build your team. Lock your roster.

A team is a roster of up to 12 players, managed by the captain through the Captain Portal.

  • The league fee is due at the time of registration to confirm your team's spot.
  • Final rosters are locked after week two.
  • All players must be at least 18 years of age.
  • The captain is responsible for collecting waivers, finalizing the roster, and submitting payment.
  • Maximum of 12 rostered players. Only rostered players may compete.
  • Minimum 6 players per match, with a maximum of 8 playing on any given day.
  • Subs must be on the roster and played in at least 1 game within Weeks 1–6 to qualify for playoffs — no late adds are allowed. Roster your subs in advance to cover schedule conflicts and player injuries.
A non-rostered player is an automatic forfeit. Keep your roster current and only play rostered players.
02 · Match format

Two rounds. Twelve games. Four points on the line.

Season 1 runs a mixed doubles round-robin — every team plays every other team across the regular season. Each league match is played over two rounds of six games each. Each round includes one women's doubles, one men's doubles, and four mixed doubles.

Start time

Weekly standings

03 · Gameplay

Games go to 11, win by 1.

First to 11 wins, must serve to win point. No cap, no win-by-two.

Championship round — win by 2

One exception: in the Week 8 championship round (the gold/silver final and the third-place match), every game is first to 11 but must be won by 2. At 10–10, play continues until a team leads by two.

Check in 30 minutes early

Be at the venue and checked in 30 minutes before the 7:00 PM start. A 10-minute grace period applies to the first game only.

Switch sides at 6

Players switch ends when the first side reaches 6 points.

Anything not covered here?

If a situation isn't spelled out in these league rules, play follows the official rulebook: the USA Pickleball Official Rulebook. When in doubt, that's the tiebreaker.

Pace matters. Most venues give us a fixed time window. Check in early and keep transitions brisk between games.
04 · Lineups

Lock early. Reveal together.

Before every match, both captains build a 12-game lineup in the Captain Portal. Each game gets two players from your roster.

Lock 1 hour ahead

Lineups must be locked on the website at least 1 hour before game time. Locked early? No problem — captains can unlock and adjust their lineup anytime until the 1-hour cutoff (as long as the opponent hasn't locked yet).

Slot rules

  • Women's doubles (G1): Both players must be women.
  • Men's doubles (G2): Both players must be men.
  • Mixed doubles (G3–G6): One man and one woman.

Rotate partners

Rotate partners each game — you may only play with the same partner once per round.

Cap on games

Maximum of 4 games per player.

Hidden simultaneous reveal

When you lock your lineup, you see your picks but nothing about the opponent. Both lineups reveal together 15 minutes before game time.

Both locked = final

Once both captains lock, lineups cannot be changed. Contact an admin for emergencies.

05 · Scoring

Home enters. Away verifies. Both sign off.

The home team enters each game's score on the shared scoresheet — you can fill in several games at once (a whole round) and tap Save. The away team reviews each score and verifies it (or flags a dispute) — they never type the scores themselves. The match counts only once both captains sign off on the final sheet.

Round points

Win the round (more games)
2 pts
Tie (3–3)
1 pt
Lose the round
0 pts

Maximum 4 points per match.

Finalizing

A match is final only when all 12 games are verified by the away team AND both captains sign off. Any edit after sign-off withdraws both sign-offs.

06 · Standings & playoffs

Six weeks to earn a seed. Two to settle it.

The 6-week regular season (plus a rivalry week) sets the table. Standings are ordered by match points earned across all league matches.

Tiebreakers

When teams are level on match points, ties break in this order:

  1. PTS — Match Points
  2. GW — Games Won
  3. PS — Points Scored
  4. DIFF — Points Differential

Playoffs — Weeks 7 & 8

The top 4 teams after Week 6 (Rivalry Week) advance to the playoffs.

  • Week 7 — Semifinals: #1 vs #4 and #2 vs #3. The bottom 2 teams play each other in a consolation matchup.
  • Week 8 — Championship: Semifinal winners meet for the championship (gold/silver); semifinal losers play for 3rd place. All championship-round games are first to 11, win by 2.

08 · Officiating

Call your own lines. Honestly.

  • Self-regulated. Games are officiated by the players on the court.
  • Sideline silence. Sideline players and fans may not make calls or coach during play.
  • Kitchen-line foot faults. First call is a warning; the second costs a point.
09 · Conduct & etiquette

Play hard. Stay human.

  • Fun, friendly competition for people who love the game.
  • Self-officiate honestly. When in doubt, the ball was in — call it against yourself.
  • Pace and grace. Keep games moving.
  • Captain handles escalations.
  • Roster only. No guest ringers.
  • No swearing, negative taunting, or poor sportsmanship.
Repeated unsportsmanlike conduct can get a team removed with no refund. We've never had to and prefer to keep it that way.
10 · Disputes

Resolve it at the court.

Scoring disagreement

If the away team disputes a score the home team entered, the system flags it. Talk courtside, agree on the correct score, and the home team re-enters it for the away team to verify.

Rule question mid-game

Pause and check the rule. If it's not in these league rules, go by the USA Pickleball Official Rulebook. If it's still unclear, replay the rally.

Need an admin?

Email the league or DM @dinksociety.pb. Try courtside resolution first.
