Rules

League rules & code of conduct

How matches are played, how scores are tallied, and the policies every Society member should know.

01

Match format

Each league night consists of one match played across two rounds, with six games per round — one women's doubles, one men's doubles, and four mixed doubles.

Games are played to 11, win by 1. The team that wins the most games in a round wins the round. Ties split the round points.

A maximum of 4 round points are available per match — 2 per round. The winner of each round earns 2 points. A tie earns 1 point each.

02

Lineups & substitutions

Captains must exchange written lineups 10 minutes before the scheduled start time. No changes are permitted after the exchange.

Substitutions may only come from the team's registered roster. The opposing captain must be notified before the round begins.

03

Scoring & standings

Division standings are determined by total round points. Tiebreakers, in order: head-to-head record, overall game differential, coin flip.

Both captains must sign the scoresheet at the end of the match, either on paper or electronically through the captain portal.

04

Roster rules

Each team may carry 4 to 10 players on their roster. The captain manages roster additions through the captain portal after initial registration.

Required player information: full name, gender, email. Optional: date of birth, DUPR rating.

Roster additions are permitted through Week 3. After Week 3, rosters are locked for the remainder of the Circuit.

05

Circuit points

At the end of each Circuit, teams earn Circuit Points based on final placement: 1st (100), 2nd (75), 3rd (50), 4th (30), 5th–6th (15).

Weekly bonuses (sweeps, perfect attendance, standout performances) stack on top. Circuit Points carry across all Circuits and feed the all-time leaderboard.

06

Code of conduct

The Dink Society is built on respect. Treat opponents, teammates, and the venue with the same energy you'd want on your own court.

  • No abusive language, threats, or unsportsmanlike behavior
  • Disputes are handled calmly through captains, not on-court arguments
  • Repeated violations may result in suspension or removal from the league
07

Forfeits & no-shows

A team that cannot field the minimum roster for a match forfeits both rounds (0 points). The opposing team is awarded a 4-0 sweep.

Captains must notify the league admin at least 24 hours in advance if a forfeit is anticipated.

08

Disputes & protests

Score disputes must be raised before both captains sign the scoresheet. Once signed, scores are final.

Rule protests can be submitted to the league admin within 24 hours of the match. The admin's decision is final.
