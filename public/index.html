How it works

A circuit, a standing, and a story.

The Dink Society runs in Circuits — each one a seven-week social league with real competition, real standings, and a drink afterward.

01 · Format

Round-robin into a gold-medal final.

Each Circuit runs six teams per division over seven weeks. Six weeks of round-robin, then championship week. Every match feeds your division standings, your stats, and your Circuit Points.

6
Teams / Division
Six teams per division. Register as a unit or sign up solo and get drafted from the free-agent pool.

Six weeks of round-robin, then a championship week. Miss a night, lose points — show up.
1
Gold title
One champion crowned per division. Winners get the medal, the bragging rights, and top of the Circuit leaderboard.
02 · A night at the Society

Two rounds. Twelve games. Four points on the line.

Each league night is one match played across two rounds, with six games per round — one women's doubles, one men's doubles, and four mixed doubles. Games go to 11, win by 1.

  • :00
    Arrive & exchange lineups

    Captains trade written lineups ten minutes before play. Confirm court assignments, get loose.

  • :15
    Round 1 · Six games

    Home serves first, away chooses side. Women's, men's, then four mixed. 2 points for the round win, 1 for a tie.

  • 1:00
    Round 2 · Six games

    Sides flip. Same structure. Another 2 points on the line.

  • 1:45
    Sign the scoresheet & stay

    Both captains sign off. Scores go straight into the Circuit leaderboard. Then it's the after-court round.
