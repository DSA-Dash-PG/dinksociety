# THE DROP — Week 2 Preview: The Big One Came Early

Somebody check the schedule-maker's coffee, because they served us the main event in Week 2. **#1 Smash Society at #2 K'CHN**, Courts 1 & 2, Monday night. Two teams walked out of opening week a combined 8–0 in match points and immediately got pointed at each other. No easing in. No appetizers. Straight to the entrée.

Here's the lay of the land before the paddles come out Monday at 7.

## The Main Event — K'CHN 🤌 vs. Smash Society 👊 (Courts 1 & 2)

This is #2 hosting #1, and on paper it's the heavyweight tilt the season needed. Both teams swept 4–0 in Week 1. The difference is in the fine print: **Smash Society took 11 of 12 games** and buried Timog Cal 131–45. **K'CHN** handled What the Dink?! 114–77 and won 9 of 12. Dominant, but a notch below Smash's demolition derby.

The subplot writes itself — K'CHN puts the league's name on the marquee (yes, *that* K'CHN), and now the house team has to defend its own kitchen against the early favorite. Home court, home name, no pressure. Smash come in as the team to beat; K'CHN come in with something to prove and the crowd on their side. Winner takes the #1 seed and the early bragging rights. This one's worth showing up for.

## Courts 3 & 6 — ZERO ZERO TWO 002 vs. Timog Cal 🌴

**002** quietly had themselves a week — a 3–1 statement over Big Dink Energy (115–62) that has them sitting third and very much in the title conversation. They're the dark horse nobody's talking about yet. **Timog Cal** are coming off the rough end of that Smash Society buzzsaw (1–11 in games, ouch) and arrive hunting their first win — or honestly, their first six games. Bounce-back energy is real, though. Don't sleep on a team with nothing to lose and a point to make.

## Courts 5 & 7 — Big Dink Energy 😤 vs. What the Dink?! 🤬

Two teams, two Week 1 losses, one very motivated Monday. Call it the First-Win Bowl. **Big Dink Energy** kept it competitive against 002 and will fancy their chances. **What the Dink?!** ran into the K'CHN sweep and would love to flip the script. Whoever wins climbs out of the early basement; whoever loses goes to 0–2 and starts doing some math. Expect this one to be scrappier than the standings suggest.

## Players to Watch — Around the League

**Smash Society 👊** — Annie K. headlines with the league's single best opener (96.4 DSR, 4–0, +35), but she's far from alone. Devin C. went a flawless 4–0 (+31) to take men's Player of the Week, and Phoebe B. matched the perfect record (+29) of her own. Add Ryan H. (also 4–0, +29) and that's *four* spotless cards on one roster — depth that just isn't fair.

**K'CHN 🤌** — Katrina N. is the house team's firestarter, a perfect 4–0 (+23, 91.0 DSR) and the one tasked with trading haymakers with Annie. Philip C. (3–1, the division's third-ranked man) and Vernice C. (3–1, +22) give K'CHN the kind of secondary scoring that wins a slugfest at home.

**ZERO ZERO TWO 002** — Shay C. (95.7 DSR, 4–0, +35) was a whisker behind Annie for the best night in the whole league and is the engine of the season's best-kept secret. Lara P. (3–1, team-high 70.1 DSR) and Kyle U. (3–1, +23) round out a roster that's quietly deeper than its #3 seed suggests.

**Big Dink Energy 😤** — Jobeth Z. refused to read the script on a 1–3 night, going 2–1 to lead the team — and teaming with Eli H. for an 11–0 pickle that was the loudest swing of their evening. Chandler H. (with Yoshi N.) banked a clutch win too. This group has a higher gear, and the basement brawl is where they find it.

**What the Dink?! 🤬** — Emanual E. owned the lone winning record on a swept roster (2–1, +9, 59.3 DSR), the best individual showing of any non-winning side all week. Veterans Elaine D. and Sheri O. teamed up to steal a women's game, and Amita P. popped a +7 frame. The wins are coming for this group.

**Timog Cal 🌴** — Ricky P. and Sandee P. combined to win Timog's *only* game of the week against the Smash buzzsaw — the single point of resistance on a 4–0 board. Noel M. pushed another game to within a point, and Aurora T. kept hers to –2 against the league's best. The record stung; the fight was already there.

---

The Drop says: Smash Society are the clear early favorite — but #1 vs. #2 on the host's court in Week 2 is exactly the kind of trap game that makes a season. We'll see you on Courts 1 & 2.
