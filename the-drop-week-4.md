## Byline
By The Society Desk

## Lead story
For three weeks, Smash Society made winning look like paperwork. Week Four made them sign in blood. Big Dink Energy marched onto the marquee court and did what nobody had managed all season, prying a point off the league leaders, before Smash steadied the ship for a 3-1 win to stay unbeaten at 7-0-1. Kai Pylkkanen was the closer the night called for, going a spotless 4-0 and leading every man in the division for points scored, with Devin Carroll (3-1, second in points) and Ryan Hom (3-1) doing the heavy lifting behind him.

Everywhere else, the contenders sent a memo written in permanent marker. ZERO ZERO TWO put What the Dink?! to the broom for a 4-0 sweep behind Player of the Week Shay C, who was untouchable at 4-0 with a plus-29 point differential that reads like a misprint. And K'CHN, quiet for a fortnight, rediscovered its kitchen game and swept Timog Cal 4-0, powered by a new-look lineup and a breakout Player of the Week performance from Jesse Nicasio.

> The penthouse still belongs to Smash. For the first time this season, though, you can hear footsteps on the stairs.

Here is the number that should keep the whole league up at night: Smash Society and ZERO ZERO TWO are both undefeated, both sitting on exactly 40 game wins, and separated by a single point of differential, 237 to 236. They have not played each other once. That changes in Week Five. Grab a seat.

## Around the League
Smash Society :: Passed their first real exam, winning 3-1 to stay perfect at 7-0-1 and hold the No. 1 seed. Kai Pylkkanen was immaculate at 4-0 and took the week's points-scored crown among men, Devin Carroll (3-1) finished second in points, and Ryan Hom (3-1), the Week Two Player of the Week, kept the net locked down. Season MVP watch: Ryan tops the entire men's DSR board at 94.9.

ZERO ZERO TWO :: A 4-0 sweep and a statement. Shay C ran the table at 4-0 to claim Player of the Week honors, the No. 1 women's DSR (93.7), and the best point differential of any woman on the night (+29). Kaithlyn R was right beside her at 4-0 (No. 2 women's DSR, 93.2, +24), while Lara P also went unbeaten and Masaki, Anthony B and Philip R all posted clean 3-0 lines. This is the deepest roster in the league, and it shows.

Big Dink Energy :: The only team all season to steal a point off the leaders, which is its own kind of trophy. Jobeth Zapata was the engine at 2-0, landing on the women's Player of the Week podium with the No. 3 DSR (91.3) and a plus-17 night. Captain Richard Hak, the Week Three Player of the Week, and Eli Henry kept the top of the lineup honest against the division's best. They sit third and firmly in the playoff picture.

K'CHN :: Back from the dead and looking like opening night, with a 4-0 sweep fueled by fresh legs. Jesse Nicasio seized Player of the Week, the No. 1 men's DSR (91.5), and the best men's point differential (+21) in a breakout debut. Chris Carag matched the energy at 3-0 (No. 2 men's DSR, 90.8, +19), and new ladies Katrina Nicasio (3-0, +21, top-five women's DSR), Elizabeth Acevedez and Jennifer Morales all stayed unbeaten. The recipe has changed, and it is working.

Timog Cal :: The scoreboard stayed cruel, but the fight did not. Ricky Pedernal remains the steadiest paddle on the roster, Noel Manansala keeps scrapping games loose, and iron-women Aurora Timbol and Selene have not missed a single game all season while Sandee Petersen keeps grinding. New arrival Bernard Comia joins ahead of Week Five, and their best shot at a first win is coming.

What the Dink?! :: Playing shorthanded with Emanual Escamilla, their steadiest hand at an even 3-3, out for the night, the newcomers stepped up. Elaine Dodson continues to lead the way, with Amita Parikh and Sheri O'Neil both refusing to make anyone's evening easy, and new face Albert Clarke making his debut in the mix. Fellow signup Grace Lee is on the roster and waiting for her first taste of action. The record is 0-8, but the roster is still taking shape.
